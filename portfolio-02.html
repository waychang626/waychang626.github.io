This academic quarter oversees the most important U.S election of our life time. As a college student in California, I, amongst many of my classmates, anxiously waits for the election to result in our favor. The democratic nominee Joe Biden and his running mate Kamala Harris have been taking a commanding double digit lead 2 weeks prior to the election day, and the Republican incumbent president Donald Trump and Mike Pence aim to convince the undecided voters that, alike the 2016 presidential election, the polls donâ€™t reflect on the actual outcome.

In this project, I aim to take a deep dive into collateral effects that stemmed from the 2020 Election. One of the most debated topics of the election of 2016 is whether to abolish the Electoral college, as the Democratic Nominee Hillary Clinton failed to win the electoral college votes while winning the popular votes. In 2020, the argument has resurged, as Donald Trump claims to have faced massive voting fraud in the electoral college, despite losing by more than 8 million popular votes. Moreover, I aim to analyse partisan behaviors that, unfortunately, emerged from this pandemic.
